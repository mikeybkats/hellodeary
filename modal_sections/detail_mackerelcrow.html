Mackerelcrow brand and e-commerce site

Mackerelcrow is an idea that was taken to it's full extreme. A brand, an e-commerce site, and a product line. How far can I take it? How far will it go?

I had been using the same backpack for about a year. It was a pretty expensive pack $200+ from a particular scandinavian brand. I was not very happy with the usability of it though. It was hard to get in and out of and not that comfortable. But it looked great. Mackerelcrow started out as some simple pencil sketches of a bagpack I envisioned.

I didn't know how to sew. But, I figured it out enough to make an extremely crude mockup. I took the mockup to some professionals and produced a few prototypes. It was an excellent experience. Although, I was paying for the service of having prototypes produced. I ended up learning about the intricacies of product development.

To make the brand even more authentic, I hired my graphic designer friend to produce a logo and woven labels. The original brand name was Mackerel and Crow. Named after two of my favorite animals. Mackerel for how tasty it is. Crows for how playful they are. James decided to marry them into one animal, the mackerelcrow. I loved it.

Further pushing on the brand we did some lifestyle photo shoots. My photography background helped with this part greatly.

The models were just some friends of mine that did it all pro bono! Really nice of them.

Now with all the products, branding stuff and photography produced, I launched an e-commerce site on the shopify platform - mackerelcrow.com

The last bit of icing on the cake is the design and build of a tradeshow booth. Mackerelcrow exhibited at the Seattle Urban Craft Uprising.
